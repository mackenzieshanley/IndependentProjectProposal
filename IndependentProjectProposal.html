PKTMAG - (Pocket Magazine Digital App)

Overview

  • For my independent project, I had the idea to create and brand a digital magazine that would be accessed through a mobile application. I chose this idea in order to refine and showcase different skills from my SMAD classes (both related to and not directly related to UX/UI), in a way that also encapsulates my personal creativity and interests/ passions outside of the classroom. The magazine and app are called ‘PKTMAG’, standing for ‘pocket magazine’, and is a fashion, music, lifestyle, and art magazine. The app serves as the host for each digital issue of the magazine that is released, as well as a creative hub for users to find inspiration and share their own ideas through a blog feature. I will be using Miro to help me create wireframes/ storyboards, along with Adobe XD to create prototypes for the app interfaces, and Photoshop and Illustrator to create a logo, moodboards, mockups, and other creative assets.

Goals

  • My overall goal for this project is to create an aesthetically pleasing, cohesive, and realistic-looking magazine brand design, with an interface that well-represents the brand's style and is user-friendly.
  • Final Deliverables:
  • Logo
  • ‘Cover’ mock-up; matching visuals (photographs, fonts, colors, graphics)
  • Example written content (1)
  • Content Research & User Research
  • Prototype showing user flow between 3-4 interface pages
  • App Specific Features
  • Users have a profile/ subscription
  • Quarterly issues of the magazine released on the platform
  • Ability to save certain stories or photographs to profile
  • Blog feature that allows magazine readers to submit their own content and responses to specific stories

Audience

  • The main target audience for this app is young to middle-aged adults who have a passion for art, music, and fashion who are looking for a platform where they can find engaging and inspiring content without having to deal with their feeds being crowded by discourse and topics they do not wish to see. The secondary user for this app would be someone who enjoys being able to access content that would usually be seen in print, from the ease of their phone.
